SỐNG
To feel, to live, to love.

A final fragment of the project.

In March 2025, I found myself overwhelmed by the pressures of club responsibilities, academics, and personal relationships. Amid that exhausting cycle, I reopened a book about a protagonist with only thirty days left to live. After reading it, I began asking myself: if I only had one day left to live, what would I choose to do?

From that inspiration, I created SỐNG, a project centered around a high school student struggling beneath the pressures of life and the relentless rhythm of the city. In many ways, the protagonist reflects myself, and shaping her journey became a process of rediscovering my own sense of self. Through the project, I came to believe that soulmates, family, and personal passions are the very things capable of transforming existence into genuine living. At the same time, the narrative quietly carries deeper emotional undercurrents - family wounds, dusty medals left forgotten over time, and unresolved feelings hidden beneath ordinary moments. One detail especially meaningful to me is the friend whom the protagonist meets by chance. The bruises left by abuse are covered with playful marker drawings by that friend - a small yet deeply compassionate gesture that gradually begins to change the protagonist herself.

Ultimately, SỐNG asks a simple yet deeply personal question: if we only had one day left to live, would we still approach the things we truly love with hesitation, regret, and the feeling that there will always be more time later?

SỐNG was an intensely personal project created with the smallest team I had ever worked with. Because of that, I chose to prioritize emotional honesty and stories drawn from my own experiences. At the same time, I wanted the models involved in the project to experience the same sense of being truly alive that I rediscovered throughout the creative process.
SỐNG faced the greatest challenge in the lack of financial resources and equipment. I filmed the project using a compact camera that had been purchased nearly twenty years ago, which meant the video quality was far from perfect. In addition, this was my first project involving cinematography, so I still lacked experience in video editing and production.
Nevertheless, what makes me most proud of SỐNG is the way its message resonated with others. Not only the model - who herself struggled with serious family issues—but also many people around me expressed deep gratitude for a project that helped them reflect on the value of life. More importantly, through creating SỐNG, I felt as though I had come back to life as well.

Through SỐNG, I realized that to live is not merely to exist, but to feel deeply, love sincerely, and remember why we began.
