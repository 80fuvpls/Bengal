Through a deeper study of painting and the life of Vincent van Gogh, I discovered that audiences in his time preferred academic art, while his impasto technique and vivid, emotional colors were often seen as rough and unrefined. This realization became the main inspiration for KHÁC BIỆT.

Looking at The Starry Night, the painting is defined by swirling blues and yellows that create a striking sky. From both our group’s perspective and my own, the contrast between the warm and cool tones not only emphasizes artistic difference but also expresses emotions that are hard to articulate. The deep blue sky carries a quiet sadness, reflecting loneliness and a sense of being lost. In contrast, the bright yellow stars light up the darkness, symbolizing joy and the strength found in pursuing one’s passion.

We wanted to express the feeling of isolation when an artist searches for their own style and encourage creatives to create their own path.

KHÁC BIỆT was an entry submitted to the annual internal competition of the Photography Club at Gia Định High School.
Throughout the process, I faced multiple challenges in execution process, marked by frequent last-minute revisions and iterative adjustments.
After two rounds of the competition-based on the judges’ evaluations and feedback, as well as the official publication on the Gia Định Photography Club’s page (ILLUSTRIS) - the entry was awarded First Prize.

Through KHÁC BIỆT, I found both skill and voice - refining how I lead, communicate, and create; unknowingly shaping a style defined by contrast and color.
