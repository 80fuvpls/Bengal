VỌNG is deeply shaped by my own personal struggles. In creating it - a project centered on emotional closure - I found myself working through a quiet sense of brokenness. It became, in many ways, my belief in a kind of love that can restore a person, and my hope for a more equal and accepting world.

At its core, VỌNG follows two individuals from completely different family backgrounds, beginning from separate points in life, yet sharing a deep emotional connection. Both carry the same longing-to love, to be loved, and to exist as their true selves. Eventually, their paths cross. In each other, they find a space to belong, and continue a journey shaped by love and quiet resilience.

At the same time, VỌNG reflects both the harshness of reality and the enduring strength of love. It is a message I hold onto: even when society tries to suppress or deny you, love and hope should not be allowed to fade. I believe that through love, people will find their way to one another-whether slowly, or by chance.

VỌNG was developed in May 2024. The idea came to me rather unexpectedly, and I chose to pursue it without hesitation. At the same time, having just completed my first year as a member of the Photography Club after Grade 10, I was eager to apply what I had learned and further refine my experience. In this project, I took on the roles of investor, creative director, and project manager.
Although this was only the second project I had undertaken, it was my first time organizing a multi-day outdoor shoot. Planning, scheduling, and time management became some of the most challenging aspects. In addition, the team consisted of peers of my age or younger, most of whom had little to no experience in artistic projects. As a result, I spent considerable time communicating, holding discussions, and organizing meetings to ensure that each shoot proceeded as planned.
The project was fortunate to receive positive feedback and strong support from the audience. Its theme also contributed, in a small way, to Pride Month 2026 (June), which led to a noticeable level of public engagement. Following the project, I was unexpectedly recognized for my ability in managing creative content. This later became a stepping stone toward my role as Vice Head of Creativity in the Photography Club.

Through VỌNG, I found a part of me that longs for freedom and love - and a quiet pull toward lives that are raw, grounded, and real.
